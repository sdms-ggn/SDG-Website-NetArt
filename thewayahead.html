The way ahead



Urban clusters are few using a very small proportion of available land. It the vast portions of barren land lying unused can be turned into housing or industrial clusters, it will promote GDP and ease the pressure on selective urban areas. This will hit the cause as it will greatly stop migration and lead to a favorable effect.




Education and Health constitute, what is called the 'Human Capital' and these are allocated a specific amonut in every year's financial budget. But their share in the budget with respect to the inflation and as a proportion of GDP is insignificant. It needs to be increased in real terms. This will lead to better education and health care services even in the villages. Educated farmers will bring in modern technology, while others may provide some other services in their villages. Thus migration the cause will stop and both the urban and the rural areas, will grow and develop.




Environment protection forms the core of sustainable development. Rapid urbanisation has adversely affected the environment in urban areas while instances like stubble burning have deteriorated the rural environment. A strong environment body is the need of the hour, which brings in strict and enforceable environment laws and acts as a strict watchdog. This by promoting water conservation, harmful gases emission, river rejuvenation, ecosystem protection, afforestation, shall lead to a healthy workforce and increased life expectancy, thereby higher GDP and inclusive and equitable development.


The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, provides a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet, now and into the future.
